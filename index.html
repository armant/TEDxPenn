TEDxPenn is a nonprofit student organization dedicated to empowerment through ideas. We hold the only license at the University of Pennsylvania to host TEDx events and conferences.

This year, we are bringing together 1000 people for a full day to engage in deep conversations about innovations and solutions with live speakers.

Nominate a speaker.

TEDxPenn, first and foremost, is about the community.
We want to showcase the ideas of the most creative, brilliant, and inspirational residents of Penn and Philly.

Now, we need your help to discover them.

If you know someone who blows your mind, someone who opens your eyes to a deep insight or a profound observation about the world and life, or someone who makes you believe that everything is possible, please tell us.

We will consider each nomination with utmost care and the most interesting candidates will be invited to an on-campus audition on January 24.

Just a few ground rules:



No Salesmanship

Talks must have no commercial agenda. Speakers should not promote their own products, books, or businesses or those of a company which employs them. The only exception is where they have specifically been invited to give a powerful product demo, or to describe the ideas in their book, and here the focus should still be on the technology and/or the ideas.
No Pseudoscience

TED and TEDx are platforms for showcasing and explaining genuine advances in science, and it's important we retain the respect of the scientific community. Speakers should avoid the misuse of scientific language to make unsubstantiated claims.
No Provocacy

Talks must not involve inflammatory political or religious agenda, nor for polarizing “us vs them” language. We seek to build consensus and provide outside-the-box thinking, not to revisit familiar, unresolvable disputes on these topics.
